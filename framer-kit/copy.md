## 01 · The *Studio*

**Statement:** The set of conditions that allow collective imagination to *arise, stay, and return.*

**Intro:** A good workshop can get a room imagining its future in one morning. The hard part is making that imagination stay — it gets written up, summarised, carried away, and the people who did the imagining are left to start from scratch next time. This studio is about building what's missing: the rhythms, spaces, rituals and archives that let imagination stick around and grow where it was made.

**01·A The studio (facts table)**

|---|---|
| Taught by | Alifiya Mutaher & Dhaval Kothari, cofounders of Lagori Collective, with Ankita Trivedi, CEPT |

| Guests | Practitioners across design, urban systems and community work |

**01·B What we're asking**
- How do people begin to *imagine together*?
- Where are futures *already being rehearsed* in South Asian cities?
- What does collective imagination need to *become durable*?
- What could help future imaginaries *gather force over time*?

**01·C The semester**
1. **Mapping systems** — Frame an inquiry inside a city's tangled systems.
2. **Field research** — Go to Bengaluru. Watch, ask, listen.
3. **Synthesis** — Find the conditions that let futures happen.
4. **Designing interventions** — Turn what you found into things that keep imagination alive.
5. **Prototypes** — Build them — tools, artefacts, rituals, platforms.
6. **Share** — Put it in front of people.

**01·D What you'll make (chips):** Participatory tools · Speculative artefacts · Rituals · Games · Digital platforms · Governance models

**Foot:** Lagori Collective is an experimental research and design studio working to keep multiple futures possible.
