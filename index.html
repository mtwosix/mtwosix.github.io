The Studio

An L3 studio at CEPT University, co-taught by Alifiya Mutaher and Dhaval Kothari (Lagori Collective) with Ankita Trivedi (Faculty of Design, CEPT). Mon, Thu & Fri · 10.30am–1.30pm IST.

Imagination infrastructure — the set of conditions that allow collective imagination to arise, stay, and return.

A good workshop can bring a room together to imagine its future in a single morning. The harder thing is making that imagination stay: visions are recorded, summarised and carried away, while the capacity that produced them rarely returns to the people who did the imagining. Communities across South Asia are invited to imagine again and again, often starting from nothing each time.

The problem is the absence of infrastructure that lets collective imagination persist, deepen, and compound where it is made — the rhythms, spaces, rituals, archives, institutions and governance forms through which people keep rehearsing, revising, and carrying forward what they have imagined together. For designers it shifts the question from what can we make to what conditions must exist for a future to be rehearsed and sustained.

01·A

The questions

  • How do people begin to imagine together?
  • Where are futures already being rehearsed in South Asian urban life?
  • What does collective imagination need to become durable?
  • What practices, rituals, institutions and governance forms could help future imaginaries gather force over time?
